As the title states, on March 7, 2022 I had a simple bilateral orchiectomy with Dr Aaron Grotas at Mt Sinai, NYC.

Why I chose him and Insurance Approval

I chose Dr Grotas because I am already in Mt Sinai's transgender clinic and he is their urologist for orchiectomy's. Additionally, my insurance (Aetna) approved him. I had problems with approval with my last insurance with him but Aetna PPO approved quickly and first try!

Approval involved an in-person visit with him, letter of support from PCP and social worker (previously mental health professional too I believe but not any longer) and then was scheduled for a month out pending insurance.

Consult

In the consult he was kind but kinda eccentric. He is outspoken about his world geo-political and economic views but whatever. Urged me towards simple not radical (inguinal) orchiectomy, and to keep scrotal tissue which I didn't really care too much about and was probably the right call. Never any bad vibes, just eccentric and busy.

Leading up to surgery

Leading up to surgery was scary even though simple orchis aren't too bad of a recovery according to most people. Labs and covid test 5 days before.

Day of surgery

Day of in the changing room he met with me and was relaxed. Mostly talked about life and stuff. Sounds like he does a lot of surgeries back to back on his surgery days - slightly worrying but that's normal I think.

Nurses were great, got on operating table, knocked out with general anesthesia. Woke up in recovery crying from happiness lol. Went home at 4pm (surgery was at 1:30 I think?), mom picked me up outside due to covid restrictions.

Complications

Minimal but more than other's I've seen. Dehiscence where the scrotum tore during surgery on day 9 that caused lots multi-day bleeding. And the long-term presence of hard tissue (fat / muscle left behind according to the doc) behind the hematoma / dehiscence that is slowly dissolving. Would love if someone could tell me what that is, I've heard other's have had it too with similar surgeries and it just takes time to dissolve.

Recovery Timeline

Stayed in hotel 1 night, pain started that night. Mostly slept. Limited mobility that night and next day. Pain got worse the next morning but nothing unbearable ever. Definitely there but manageable. Just used OTC 400mg tylenol I believe. Should have iced more that first day but the gauze and compression underwear they give you prevented it from doing anything.

Pretty much back to normal, still being careful, the hematoma is long gone but there is still hard sometimes tender tissue remaining on the right side that shouldn't be there and is slowly going away. That will probably take a few weeks - months to fully dissolve.

Day 30

Day 43 (today)

Feeling good. Definitely still have to be careful how I handle the area, but am able to touch it and feel it safely and am basically doing everything normally. Much less hard tissue remaining, but still quite a bit, maybe half what was there.

General Thoughts

Glad I did it, but it was more of a recovery than I expected after having seen other people's super quick recovery. I had lots of bruising and swelling, dehiscence, and long term hard swelling/tissue left behind. Overall, Dr Grotas is good, not amazing imo, but then again surgery and healing is complicated and a good surgeon can have complications. Wasn't thrilled with the level of attention he gave me but he was available when I asked. Tucking is way easier, but will get even more so as the hard tissue dissolves. That alone is worth it

I already was off spiro / AA, so this was really more cosmetic than anything else lol. Overall glad I did it. A little scared for future surgeries given how prone to bruising and swelling I was. Not sure if that's a valid fear

Recommendations

Buy / Get:

  • Period / post-partum panties. These worked great for me
  • Donut pillow
  • Sweatpants
  • ICE PACKS
  • Wet Wipes
  • Neosporin
  • Surgical Tape (didn't use)
  • Steri-strips (did use, you probably won't need them)
  • Metamucil / Miralax (didn't use)
  • Gauze pads and non-stick pads (bandaid makes good ones)
  • At least a week off work if not at home
  • Friend to help you out with lifting things

You don't need a peri-bottle or pan to bed wash in in my experience
