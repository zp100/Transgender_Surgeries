I wanted to wait six months after surgery and then post, but I'm probably going to delete my account and never return to this hellsite. I'm honestly tired of it and the immense drama that it brings. Nevertheless.... onto my review.

TL;DR - I am presently happy with my results and would do it again.

I'm about 2.5 months out of surgery at this point.

​

Bedside Manner

Shih, Kleinberger, and their staff were all super nice and helpful. Responded to my messages and questions pretty fast pre- and post-operation. Shih called me the day after I left the hospital to make sure everything was going okay.

I received prompt responses to my post-surgery concerns (and there were a lot of them, I've never been more nervous about fucking something up).

Follow-up appointment went well

So as far as bedside manner is concerned A+.

​

Overall Results

I'm still a little swollen, but the majority of it is gone and I'm feeling great. I'm already much happier with my face than I was pre-surgery. I had a fairly prominent brow, orbitals, and nose - those have all been corrected to a pretty satisfactory level. I now have a very nice forehead shape with no humps, and practically no shadowing on my eyes. My jaw has also been tapered, but that one is a bit harder to tell as my jaw is definitely the most swollen part of my face, and ongoing electrolysis certainly doesn't help hah. My nose is the only part I wish they went a little harder on, but it's still definitely feminine - and to be honest I told them I didn't want a huge amount of work done on it, so that's on me.

​

Incisions

All my incisions have been healing without complications, and fairly rapidly IMO. I guess you could take that as a merit toward their sewing skills. At this point they're all healed to the degree they won't reopen, and have flattened out considerably. There's still a little pigment, but over time that should diminish.

​

Numbness

So far, I've gotten a lot of feeling back in my surgical areas:

  • Top portion of my scalp is still numb
  • Lower regions of scalp currently regaining feeling
  • Forehead is almost to 100%, the new nerve endings in some areas are a little too sensitive, should dull over time
  • Nose area at 100%
  • Jaw is maybe around half way there, chin is still kinda numb

​

A couple recovery tips:

  • Eat. A lot. You might feel sick to your stomach from the opioids, but the more you eat the faster you heal. Pureed soups and protein drinks are great for this.
  • Try not to take the opioids. They've been shown to slow healing, make you queasy, and have well-documented addiction problems. Outside of maybe the first day out of the hospital, my pain was easily managed with the acetaminophen they gave me. In fact I would say I was basically rocking 0/10 pain
  • They give you an inverse spirometer to use and tell you how to use it. Definitely do that, it will help with your breathing and honestly, anxiety.
  • Be very diligent with icing, I'm positive the reason I didn't get major swelling is due in part to following the icing instructions to the letter. They'll give you a lot of ice packs and some handy slings for them - if they don't, be sure to request them when they come in the next morning to show you how to swap your bandages
  • I found taking anti-swelling supplements (may have) helped a lot. I was taking Turmeric, Bromelain, and Arnica. I think the arnica helped the most, as I missed a few doses at one point and my swelling was more noticeable. As always, check with your doctor to see if this is a good idea for you.

​

Things I didn't expect, which had nothing to do with the surgery:

  • That first overnight in the hospital was horrible! Wake up from anesthesia all fucked up, can't breathe through your nose, you're all bandaged up and then the swelling starts and it gets super claustrophobic and and and... Because of COVID you can't have visitors, and as this was my first major surgery I admit I was pretty scared effectively being alone. The nurses were all wonderful though and very attentive.
  • I read about people having post-surgery regrets a week out, like a buyer's remorse kind of thing. I definitely wasn't prepared for just how rough that was. It hit me on Saturday, about four days out of the hospital. It was definitely a combination of having to be off my hormones for two weeks (I do injections so I couldn't just stop a few days before), my mom having to head back home, and just being overwhelmed. That was a very hard night.
  • When they say you need a helper those first few days, you NEED a helper. It would have been immensely more challenging if I had to get through that myself.
  • Your hair is going to be disgusting, you're going to have a ton of ointment and aquaphor in it. Get a nice cleansing shampoo
  • THE ITCHING. As your scalp et al begin to heal, the nerves will itch. A lot. And there's nothing you can do about it. You can't itch it because it's numb. Best I found was just applying gentle pressure to the itching areas - just lightly place your palm on the area.

​

Shopping List?

  • Food
    • Protein Drinks (I had about four a day to make sure I was getting enough protein)
    • Electrolyte drinks
    • Soft foods, even soups may be too thick to chew and swallow however.
  • Medications/Supplements (Ask Dr.)
    • Arnica cream and tablets
    • Bromelain
    • Extra tube or two of Bacitracin.
      • NOT Neosporin, that can cause dermatitis if used repeatedly and you will be using it repeatedly
    • They recommend getting extra elastic bandages and gauze. To be honest they gave me so much I never even got through everything they provided me with, let alone my extras
    • Aquaphor
    • Multivitamin
      • Your diet is gonna be fucked and you won't get proper nutrition for a couple weeks
    • BCAAs
      • As above, amino acids are extremely beneficial in muscle and tissue repair. You won't be getting enough from your diet.
  • Supplies
    • Straws
    • Blender
    • A wedge pillow for sleeping, much easier than stacking four pillows
    • Humidifier - The extra moisture helps the nose recover faster
    • Headphones?
      • Your ears are going to be constantly covered by bandages, so there's no getting earbuds in or out.
    • Shirts you can easily put on and take off. Your head is gonna be swollen so plan accordingly
      • I used cheap loose buttons ups
    • Children's size toothbrush
      • Once you can brush your teeth again, it's still going to be pretty swollen and tender. The smaller the head the better
    • Cotton swabs
      • For application of ointment to various places
    • Tissues
      • NOT FOR BLOWING NOSE only dab
    • Sensitive Baby wipes
      • For cleaning gunk off your face because you otherwise can't really wash it
      • Don't underestimate the psychological benefits of feeling clean
    • A puppy
      • Okay don't like, go get a dog
      • But mine is very cute and comforting
    • Patience

I hope this helps. I hope things go well for you. I hope you're happy.
