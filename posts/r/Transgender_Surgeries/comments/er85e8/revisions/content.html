It's hard to tell and I may be wrong, but I don't think revisions are all that common except with Suporn patients (they appear to go in greater numbers for smaller reasons).

Unless you're looking at Suporn I think its better to try and find a surgeon where you're less likely to need a revision. It may depend on the surgeon. With some surgeons functional revisions are included within a specific time, around a year. For example if there is excessive hooding limiting sensation, or there are restrictions with urine flow. It may be a good idea to look up what they specify.

Otherwise many surgeons do revisions, it may be an idea to make a few photos and ask what their opinion would be. Asking how much experience they have with revisions, and how it might affect sensation may be a good idea.
