He said he took off as much as he possibly could without damaging my vocal cords. He uses what I believe is the standard technique where they stick a very fine needle in to mark how far they can safely shave. I had no problems with voice loss or anything after.

Recovery was very easy, I showed up in the morning, procedure was less than an hour, and went home that afternoon (accompanied by my partner). they prescribed some codeine which I took day of and the day after surgery, but otherwise not much pain, just discomfort. I had surgery on a Thursday and started a new job on monday, so very easy recovery (although I did wear turtlenecks for a little bit).

The scar isn't bad, some days it's more visible than others, i believe eventually it will be pretty much invisible. As mentioned above, I wish it was under my chin instead of the front of my neck, but what can you do.

I still have a somewhat visible adams apple unfortunately, but I don't think that reflects on him as a surgeon, I just had a fairly pronounced one to begin with and am skinny so it sticks out. But still it's much better than it used to be.

I had a trach shave with him this October. I'm healing nicely and was very happy with the experience and the results. I was very concerned about damage to my voice since I'm a trained singer, love to do impressions, may get into voice acting. He was very sensitive to this and gave me a great result and despite some hoarseness for a little bit (which has cleared up) I still have access to all the parts of my voice I had before.

My scar is still pretty visible since it's only been 2 months and I don't really have any natural folds to hide the incision in (recessed chin... my neck pretty much goes straight into my chin without a fold). But even now at 2 months it's easy to hide with makeup. I'm not out to all my family and have made it through multiple gatherings without anyone noticing the scar.

Would recommend!
