I don't think they shaved my hairline, no.

The area was incredibly swollen for the first week or so. I looked like an alien. Not painful though.

I think bangs could be a good option for hiding it - yeah. There is gonna be a scar where they do the coronal incision, and if you got to a hairstylist that will probably be somewhat obvious to them for a long time.

The aftercare only feels a bit intense immediately after, but nurses will come and help and instruct you. I was very happy that I had my mom come along, so she could help me clean the area. You have to keep the area away from direct sunlight for a while, and summers coming up, so that parts a little annoying.. I had mine done in April, and I just wore a cap and used sunscreen religiously.

I think it took about a year before the redness faded, and the area looked normal. I think it gradually looks more and more natural with time, as your hair line stops being 'too' perfect.

I'd say that the thickness is the same. I have naturally curly hair, with somewhat different textures on different parts of my head. So since the transplant was grafted from the top of my scalp, it does have a slightly different texture and curl than the other hair in the front. I get keratin treatments now and straighten my hair, so the end result is that it all looks the same now.

I also got rhino, lip-lift, forehead. I have been very happy with all of it. The swelling on the nose took a while to subside and the lip-lift settled and looked natural after a few months. Nothing has been painful. Although I'll say that the first few days are pretty uncomfortable - but after that it's mostly smooth sailing.

FT has been really wonderful about answering questions and reassuring worries afterwards.
