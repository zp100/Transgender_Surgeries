Hi! I know your post is a few years old by now so you may already have the answers you're looking for, but I wanted to share just in case. I am almost 5 weeks post op with Dr. Gast. I had a zero depth vaginoplasty (also called a vulvoplasty I believe). Everything down there is about half as swollen as it was when I first left the hospital but it's still pretty puffy so I'm not sure I can help with photos. But even with the swelling I can tell the end result is going to look really nice. I can only speak to my personal experience but I loved Dr. Gast and her team. I felt extremely safe and cared for while I was there. I honestly didn't want to leave the hospital because my support system back home is not the best. I did have complications post op with urination. I had to get driven back to UW Health 3 days post op to get a catheter put in because I couldn't pee. It was a difficult car ride because I was in a lot of pain and live 2 hours away. I think it was a combination of being so swollen and also having blood clots in my urine. (My pee literally looked like a jar of blood after getting the catheter put in). Since then everything has been okay though. I got the catheter out and am on antibiotics for a UTI but am in no pain and can move around better everyday. As far as the pictures, I'm not sure if it's a UW Health policy or something, but they may have pictures they can show you when you go in for your consultation. I thought I remembered someone mentioning that when I was there. I originally planned on getting both the vaginoplasty and breast augmentation together, but decided against the breast augmentation about a week before my surgery. I got kinda sketched out by reading stories of breast implant illness. I also have facial feminization appointments scheduled in a few months. I'll be getting surgery on my nose and laser hair removal completed. Those are scheduled through Dr. Chaiet's office.
