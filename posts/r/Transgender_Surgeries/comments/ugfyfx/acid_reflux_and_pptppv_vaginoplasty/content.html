It really shouldn’t be. I have acid reflux and got peritoneal flap vaginoplasty without any issues. Your stomach and peritonaeum are different things. Maybe if you’ve had abdominal surgery for reflux, the adhesions caused by it could make another surgery entering through the abdominal cavity more challenging, but you could probably find a doctor willing to do it. Usually it takes several abdominal surgeries before surgeons will reject you because of the risk adhesions pose.
