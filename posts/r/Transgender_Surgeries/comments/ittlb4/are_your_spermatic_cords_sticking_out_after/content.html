A completely "flat" presentation won't be present, but it has nothing to do with the spermatic cords and a "radical" orchi won't make any difference.

Unless you remove all the scrotum (a scrotectomy, which you do not want to do if you plan on GRS), you will always have some protrusion "down there".

I had a successful simple orchi. Quick healing, low pain, and the scar was basically invisible after a few months. I couldn't really feel anything 'dangling'. I couldn't feel much of anything inside. I don't know how much of the cords were left behind, but I certainly couldn't feel them inside the scrotum anymore.

True, the tissue wasn't completely flat, but it wasn't as dangling as before, either. The scrotal tissue is quite flexible, so I could stretch it if needed, but most of the time it was neat and compact. Definitely increased ease of tucking.

I stretched the scrotum daily, on my doctor's orders, to prepare for SRS. When I stretched the tissue, it felt like just skin and nothing else. I could essentially flatten out the skin completely, without detecting any contents inside. I hope that answers your question about 'dangling cords'.
