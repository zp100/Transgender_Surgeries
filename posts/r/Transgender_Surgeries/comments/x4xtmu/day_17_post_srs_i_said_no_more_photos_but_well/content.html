It may be necrosis, but there have been a lot of people come through this sub with various levels of the same and things turned out fine. It sounds like your treatment team and your surgeon are communicating to get you the best result with the least intervention. Adding more surgery trauma to this may not be the best solution. Regardless, this isn't what you anticipated, nor is it pleasant, and that's the most difficult part of this kind of post-surgery experience -making the adjustments and simply not knowing what to expect. You'll get through this. It sounds like you have quality healthcare and open communication with your surgeon. Give in to the healing process, and follow their instructions. There are a lot of phases to healing from SRS. It's a slow rollercoaster at best as your body adjusts to everything. You aren't in terrible shape for 2 1/2 weeks out.
